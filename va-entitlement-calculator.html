VA Entitlement Calculator

Compute remaining entitlement, the zero-down loan ceiling, and any required down payment from COE figures and the current county loan limit. Pulls 2026 conforming limits as presets.

Inputs

All figures from the borrower's Certificate of Eligibility.

2026 FHFA one-unit conforming limit for the subject property's county. Baseline ($832,750) applies in most counties; high-cost counties scale up to the $1,249,125 ceiling, which is also the limit used in Alaska, Hawaii, Guam, and the USVI. Enter the exact county figure for limits between the two.

From the COE line "Total Entitlement Charged to Previous VA Loans." Generally 25% of any outstanding prior VA loan principal, or the original guaranty amount on a prior loan still tying up entitlement.

Optional. If supplied, the panel calculates any required down payment above the zero-down ceiling.

With full entitlement, no county cap applies. VA guarantees 25% of whatever loan amount the lender approves; zero down is available at any price point. Borrower ceiling is set by income, credit, and DTI — not entitlement math.

The math

  1. Max potential guaranty = county limit × 25%
  2. Remaining entitlement = max guaranty − entitlement charged
  3. Zero-down loan ceiling = remaining entitlement × 4
  4. Required down payment = (purchase price − zero-down ceiling) × 25% when price exceeds the ceiling, otherwise $0.
