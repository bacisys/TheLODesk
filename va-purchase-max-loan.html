The math & the rule

  1. Base value = lesser of sales price and appraised value.
  2. Required guaranty is taken on the value plus the financed funding fee. For a note ≤ $144,000 it is tiered — 50% up to $45,000; flat $22,500 from $45,001–$56,250; 40% above, capped at $36,000. For a note > $144,000 it is a flat 25%.
  3. Available guaranty: with full entitlement, VA guarantees 25% of the loan at any amount — no county cap, no down payment. With partial / remaining entitlement, available = 25% × county loan limit − entitlement already used (notes ≤ $144k use $144,000 → the $36,000 basic entitlement). Full entitlement and unrestored prior use are mutually exclusive.
  4. Down payment = required guaranty − available guaranty, when positive. This is the guaranty shortfall the Veteran must cover.
  5. Base loan = lesser value − down payment; funding fee = base loan × factor; note amount = base loan + financed funding fee; LTV = note ÷ lesser value.
  6. Funding fee factors current per VA Circular 26-23-06 (loans closed on/after 4/7/2023): purchase first use 2.15% / subsequent 3.30% at <5% down, 1.50% at 5–9.99%, 1.25% at 10%+; exempt 0%.

Estimates only. Your AUS findings, the VA Certificate of Eligibility, investor overlays, and lender max loan amount are authoritative. Closing costs and prepaids are separate from the down payment shown here. Confirm current funding fee factors and county limits before relying on a figure.
