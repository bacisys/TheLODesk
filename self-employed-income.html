Self-Employed Income

Qualifying income from the tax returns the way underwriting runs it — Schedule C, Partnership (1065), S-Corp (1120S), and C-Corp (1120) — using Fannie Mae Form 1084 / Freddie Mac Form 91 add-backs, then the averaging and flags for your loan program.

The method & the program rules

  1. Each year's adjusted business income starts from the tax return's bottom line and applies the Fannie 1084 / Freddie 91 treatment: add back non-cash deductions (depreciation, depletion, amortization, casualty loss, business-use-of-home) and subtract non-cash / nonrecurring items (nonrecurring income or gains, the nondeductible meals portion, short-term notes payable).
  2. Vehicle depreciation inside the standard mileage deduction is added back as business miles × the IRS depreciation factor for that year (2025 = $0.33, 2024 = $0.30, 2023 = $0.28, 2022/2021 = $0.26, 2026 = $0.35).
  3. For partnerships and S-corps, K-1 amounts are already the borrower's share; business-return add-backs are multiplied by ownership %. S-corp and C-corp add the borrower's W-2 wages from the entity. C-corp income is corporate income net of tax, times ownership %.
  4. The income is averaged over 24 months when stable or rising. If the most recent year is lower, the tool uses the most-recent-year figure and flags it — Fannie/Freddie require documenting the trend; FHA and VA generally require the more conservative figure and a 2-year history.
  5. Flags surface the cases underwriting cares about: declining income, <25% ownership, a business loss, and (for K-1 income) whether distributions are supported by business liquidity.

Estimates only. Final qualifying income is determined by the underwriter from complete tax returns, transcripts, AUS findings, and program guidelines (Fannie B3-3.2/3.3/3.4, Freddie 5304, FHA 4000.1, VA Lenders Handbook Ch. 4). This tool does not store anything you enter.
