Self-Employed Income

Qualifying income built line-for-line on the agency cash-flow worksheet (Fannie Mae Form 1084 / Freddie Form 91), for Schedule C, 1065, 1120S, and 1120. Pick the program \u2014 Fannie, Freddie, FHA, VA, or USDA \u2014 for the right averaging and rules; the tax-return add-backs are the same for all.

Inputs

Pick the program and business type, then enter the line items for two years.

Loan program

Same Form 1084 add-backs for every program — the selector changes the averaging and the program-specific rules & flags.

Business structure

Below 25% is generally not treated as self-employed. For partnerships and S-corps the business-return adjustments are multiplied by this share (K-1 amounts are already your share); a C-corp's income is used only at 100% ownership.

The method & the program rules

  1. Each year's adjusted cash flow is built on Fannie Mae Form 1084. Start with the return's bottom line, then add back the non-cash deductions \u2014 depreciation, depletion, amortization/casualty loss, business use of home \u2014 and subtract nonrecurring income and the non-deductible travel & meals exclusion (Schedule C: lines 3a\u20133g; B3-3.6-03).
  2. Vehicle mileage (Form 1084 line 3d): when the standard mileage deduction is used, the depreciation portion is added back as business miles \u00d7 the IRS factor for that year \u2014 2026 = $0.35, 2025 = $0.33, 2024 = $0.30, 2023 = $0.28, 2022/2021 = $0.26.
  3. Partnerships (1065) and S-corps (1120S): K-1 ordinary income, net rental, and guaranteed payments are already your share and are not weighted (lines 7 / 9). The business-return adjustments (depreciation, depletion, amortization, \u2212notes payable <1yr, \u2212travel/entertainment) are summed and \u00d7 your ownership % (lines 8 / 10). S-corp adds your W-2 from the entity.
  4. C-corp (1120): taxable income \u2212 total tax + the add-backs \u2212 notes payable \u2212 travel/entertainment. Under Fannie 1084 this is taken less dividends paid to you and only at 100% ownership (line 11). Under Freddie Form 91 \u00a710 the corporate subtotal is instead weighted by your ownership % with no dividend subtraction \u2014 the selector handles this automatically. Either way, add your W-2 from the corporation.
  5. Form 91 Section I \u2014 other 1040 schedules. Freddie's Schedule Analysis Method sums every recurring income schedule on the personal return, not just the business. The optional Other Form 1040 income drawer captures Schedule B (recurring interest & dividends, less any 1120 dividends already counted), a second Schedule C, Schedule D recurring capital gains, Schedule E royalties, and Schedule F farming. These are personal items, summed at 100% (never ownership-weighted). Net rental income on Schedule E uses Form 92, not this tool.
  6. Averaging by program (same add-backs, different combine). Fannie, Freddie, VA, USDA use the 24-month average and analyze the trend (a decline is flagged to document, not auto-substituted). FHA instead qualifies on the lower of the 24-month average and the most-recent year (HUD 4000.1 II.A). VA adds a separate residual-income test; USDA splits repayment income (qualifying, with add-backs) from annual household income for the 115%-of-AMI eligibility cap.
  7. K-1 distributions are not income. Ordinary income counts only if distributed or supported by business liquidity; cash distributions themselves establish access, not qualifying income (Form 1084 instructions; Freddie 5304.1(e)). Flags surface declines, <25% ownership, loss years, and the distribution/liquidity test.

Estimates only. Final qualifying income is determined by the underwriter from complete tax returns, transcripts, AUS findings, and program guidelines (Fannie Mae Form 1084 & Selling Guide B3-3.5\u2013B3-3.7; Freddie Mac Form 91 & Guide 5304.1). This tool does not store anything you enter.
