The math & the rule

  1. New P&I = amortize(new loan amount, new rate, new term). For a fixed-to-ARM, the new ARM's initial rate is used.
  2. Net tangible benefit — rate test fixed→fixed: new rate ≤ current − 0.50%; fixed→ARM: new rate ≤ current − 2.00%. ARM→fixed qualifies on structure (stability), with recoupment/no-cost still required.
  3. Statutory recoupment = (recoupable fees + points cost) ÷ monthly P&I savings — must be ≤ 36 months. Taxes, escrow, and the VA funding fee are excluded from this numerator.
  4. Comparison-statement recoupment = (all fees incl. taxes, escrow & funding fee) ÷ monthly P&I savings — the borrower-facing figure; informational.
  5. Discount-point LTV cap ≤ 1 point → LTV ≤ 100%; > 1 point → LTV ≤ 90% (new loan amount ÷ appraised value).
  6. Seasoning ≥ 210 days from 1st payment due AND ≥ 6 monthly payments made.
  7. If P&I does not decrease (e.g., ARM→fixed or term reduction), recoupment can't be met — the IRRRL must be structured with no fees, closing costs, or expenses other than taxes, escrow, and the funding fee.

Basis: 38 U.S.C. § 3709 and VA Circular 26-19-22 (Change 1). This tool estimates the statutory tests; your investor's comparison statement, AUS findings, and any overlays are authoritative. Recoupment timing references the statutory framework — confirm the lender's reference point (loan issuance vs. first payment due date) per current guidance.
